Vinyl Recycling

Giving new life to old records - sustainability meets music

Our Commitment to Sustainability

At Vinyl Record House, we believe in preserving not just music, but also our planet. Our vinyl recycling program transforms damaged or unwanted records into new opportunities while reducing environmental impact.

What is Vinyl Recycling?

Vinyl recycling is an eco-friendly process that repurposes old or damaged vinyl records, reducing waste and environmental impact. Instead of ending up in landfills, these records get a second chance at life.

By recycling vinyl, we can:

  • Reduce plastic waste in landfills
  • Create unique decorative items and art pieces
  • Support sustainable business practices
  • Preserve resources for future generations

How Our Recycling Program Works

Simple steps to give your old vinyl a new purpose

1
Bring Your Records

Bring your damaged, warped, or unwanted vinyl records to our store. We accept records that are beyond repair for playing.

2
Assessment

Our team evaluates each record to determine the best recycling method and potential for creative repurposing.

3
Creative Transformation

Records are transformed into art pieces, bowls, decorative items, or components for new vinyl production.

4
New Life

The recycled items find new homes as unique art pieces or contribute to sustainable vinyl production.

Benefits of Vinyl Recycling

Environmental Impact

Reduces plastic waste and minimizes the environmental footprint of vinyl production by reusing existing materials.

Creative Art

Transforms damaged records into unique decorative pieces, wall art, and functional items for music lovers.

Community Impact

Supports eco-conscious consumers and builds a community around sustainable music appreciation.

What We Accept for Recycling

We Accept:
  • Scratched or damaged vinyl records
  • Warped records that won't play properly
  • Records with significant wear and tear
  • Incomplete or partial albums
  • Records without sleeves or covers
We Don't Accept:
  • Records in playable condition (we'd rather sell these!)
  • Rare or valuable records (let us evaluate first)
  • Records with toxic materials or chemicals
